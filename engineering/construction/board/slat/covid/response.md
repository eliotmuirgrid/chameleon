# Slatboard and COVID response

This kind of flexibility matters most when the system is under stress. COVID-19 surges made that visible: when demand spiked, many hospitals could not expand or reconfigure fast enough.

That lack of adaptability fed broader social consequences, including delayed care, isolation, economic hardship, and long ripple effects on mental and physical health.

The point is not blame. The design lesson is that system architecture has human consequences.
