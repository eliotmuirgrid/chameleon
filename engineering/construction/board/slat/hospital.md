# Slatboard (detail) - Healthcare environments

Hospitals do extraordinary work, but many are constrained by rigid systems that quietly drive up cost and limit adaptation.

Something as basic as setting up a hospital bed can become complex and expensive, not only because of care itself, but because of logistics, fixed installations, and tightly coupled equipment around it.

Slatboard-style thinking suggests a more hopeful direction: treat the environment as an open, modular system where equipment, monitors, power, and storage can be mounted, moved, and reconfigured without rebuilding rooms every time needs change.

For COVID-specific system pressure and response lessons, see [COVID response](./covid/response.md).

The point is not blame. The design lesson is that system architecture has human consequences.

When healthcare environments are modular and easier to evolve, teams can respond faster in crises, reduce waste, and lower burden on patients and society.
