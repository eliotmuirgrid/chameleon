# Buy a flowering plant instead of cut flowers

**Summary:** If you want **flowers** in the home, a useful default is to **buy a potted plant that flowers** instead of a **cut bouquet** when you are choosing what to pay for. The potted option is a **living plant** that can **bloom again**; cut flowers are a **short, one-way** use of the stems.

---

You still trade **care** (water, light, maybe repotting) for **duration**. The point of the page is the **purchase shape**: **plant with flowers** vs **flowers without plant**.

**Transfer test:** the same “buy the durable thing that produces the experience” move shows up in **herbs in pots** vs **one-off bunches** from the store—see [Herbs you eat as a first win (gardening)](bottlenecks_herb_you_eat.md).
