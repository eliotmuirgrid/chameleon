# How this all started

## What a concepts site is (independent of technology)

A **concepts site** is a **set of short pages**: **one main idea** per page, **linked** so readers can **follow** a path. The point is **clarity**, **reuse**, and a **hierarchy** that matches **how people learn**—**small** notes that **add up** to a bigger picture.

That **idea** does **not** belong to any **one** product. You could host it as a **wiki**, a **folder of Markdown files**, a **book**, or something else. **Technology** is **how** you implement it; **structure** is **what** you are building.

---

## How the original concepts site was hosted (Confluence)

**Here**, the first home for much of this material was **Atlassian Confluence**—a **browser wiki** used at **Interfaceware**. What Confluence *is*, and why it behaves like a **closed** system, is its own note: [Confluence](pareto/general/confluence.md).

The **space** we started from:

- [Interfaceware Confluence overview](https://interfaceware.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/EC/overview?homepageId=2023260241)

Confluence was **easy** to **start** in, but **linking** pages, **restructuring** when ideas moved, and **treating** the text as **simple, portable** material got **harder** over time—see [open vs closed](pareto/general/open_versus_closed.md). That **friction** is **about the tool**, not about the **concepts-site** idea itself.

---

## What we use now (same idea, different implementation)

This repository keeps the **same** kind of **small, linked** concept pages, but as **Markdown** under [source control](pareto/general/source_control.md) implemented with **[Git](computer/git/git.md)**, with **AI** often helping draft and edit—see [How I write these pages](tools/ai/B.How/do/I/write/these/pages.md). The **move** was for **openness**, **history**, and **workflow**—not because the **ideas** changed.

The **old** wiki stack was [Confluence](pareto/general/confluence.md). **Current** building blocks are [Markdown](pareto/general/markdown.md), [source control](pareto/general/source_control.md), and [Git](computer/git/git.md). **Why** plain files matter: [Open systems vs closed systems](pareto/general/open_versus_closed.md).
