# Decompression

**Summary:** **Decompression** is taking something held in a **dense** form and **spreading it out** into a **working layout** so you can **see**, **sort**, **use**, or **fix** it.

---

## Where it shows up

- **Physical:** Emptying a **tool closet** onto **two tables** so every item is visible and reachable.
- **Digital:** Expanding an **archive**, **checkout**, or **export** so files or records are **laid out** instead of packed in one bundle.
- **Cognitive:** Writing thoughts on cards or a whiteboard so they can be **moved and grouped**—the same “make it addressable” move.

The pattern is always: **compressed storage** → **expanded workspace** → **work** → (often) **pack back** or **discard**.

---

## Why it works

Dense packing **saves space** but **hides** structure. Decompression **buys visibility** and **access** at the cost of **temporary sprawl**. You accept a **larger footprint** on purpose so **search** and **decisions** get easier in the moment.

---

## Pair with decluttering

[Decluttering and retrieval](decluttering_and_retrieval.md) is about **long-term** [**entropy**](../../system/design/entropy.md) and **finding things** in a smaller, clearer collection—fewer places to look **after** the work. **Decompression** is often a **short-term** move: you **inflate** the search space **on purpose** to **finish a job**—then you **re-pack**, **donate**, or **reorganize** so the next retrieval stays sane. [Small packet](../../system/design/small_packet.md) thinking is the opposite instinct sometimes—**small units** in motion—while decompression is the **spread-out** phase when a packet is **opened**.
