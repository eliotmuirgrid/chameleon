# Salsa

**Summary:** **Salsa** names a **wide family** of **Afro-Latin** popular music and the **social partner dance** that grew with it, especially from **Cuban** roots (son, mambo) and **Puerto Rican** and **Nuyorican** (New York) scenes in the **mid-20th** century. In one line: **music you can move to**, built on a **danceable** groove and, very often, a **clear rhythmic spine** the band and the dancers can share.

---

## Concept

A **salsa** track is still **dance music first**: a **repeating** harmonic loop, **percussion-forward** mix, and **call-and-response** phrasing that makes it easy to **hear the one** and stay **with** the group. The **dance** side is a **slot** or **circular** social style: **lead** and **follow** interpret the same **pulse**; good dancing often looks like the pair is **trading and recovering balance** on every phrase: **equilibrium** in motion, as in [Create space in partner dance](../dance/create/space.md).

**Transfer test:** the same **shared pulse** and **turn-taking** show up in **jazz** jam sessions, **choir** singing, and **team** sports: many people, **one** clocked rhythm.
