# Lawyer example

A lawyer can create bad communication by assuming the client understands legal strategy when the client is actually confused and scared.

Example: the client asks, "Will I lose my home?" and the lawyer replies with dense terms about jurisdiction, procedural posture, and indemnity clauses, but never answers the core fear.

This style is bad because it sounds expert but misses the client’s real question, emotional state, and decision needs.

A better style is: answer the client’s plain question first, then explain legal detail in simple steps tied to what the client must do next.
