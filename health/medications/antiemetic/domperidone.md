# Domperidone

## What it is

Domperidone is an anti-nausea/prokinetic medicine.

It is used to reduce nausea and vomiting and to improve gastric emptying in selected patients.

## How we think it might work

Domperidone blocks peripheral dopamine (`D2`) receptors.

This can reduce nausea signaling and improve stomach motility.

## Typical dosage ranges (general)

Dose depends on indication, age, cardiac risk profile, and local prescribing guidance.

Common adult oral patterns in practice:

- Often `10 mg` per dose.
- Commonly up to three times daily for short courses, based on clinician guidance.

Practical timing notes:

- Often advised before meals.
- Use the shortest duration and lowest effective dose.

Maximum daily dose and duration should follow the exact product label and clinician instructions.

## Known negative side effects

Common side effects:

- dry mouth,
- abdominal discomfort,
- headache or dizziness.

Important risks:

- QT prolongation/arrhythmia risk (higher with high doses, older age, heart disease, or interacting medicines),
- elevated prolactin (for example breast tenderness, galactorrhea, menstrual changes; less commonly sexual dysfunction).

Urgent warning symptoms:

- fainting, palpitations, chest pain, or severe dizziness,
- facial swelling, wheeze, or other signs of allergic reaction.

## Important safety notes

- This is general information, not a personal treatment plan.
- Dosing should be set by a licensed clinician.
- Check interactions carefully (especially medicines that prolong QT interval or strongly inhibit CYP3A4).
- Avoid use without clinician guidance in known heart rhythm disorders.
