# Clotrimazole

## What it is

Clotrimazole is an antifungal medicine, usually used topically on skin.

It treats common fungal infections such as athlete's foot, ringworm, and candidal skin rash.

## How we think it might work

Clotrimazole disrupts fungal cell membrane production, which weakens and stops fungal growth.

## Typical dosage ranges (general)

Topical antifungal dosing is usually by frequency and duration, not oral milligrams.

Common use pattern in practice:

- apply a thin layer to affected skin `1-2` times daily,
- continue for the full recommended course even if symptoms improve early,
- duration is often a few weeks depending on infection type/site.

## Known negative side effects

Common local side effects:

- mild burning, stinging, itching, or redness,
- local irritation or dryness.

Less common but important:

- allergic contact dermatitis,
- worsening rash if diagnosis is incorrect or mixed skin condition is present.

Sexual side effects:

- Sexual side effects are not a common primary effect of topical clotrimazole.

## Important safety notes

- This is general information, not a personal treatment plan.
- Use as directed by a licensed clinician or product label.
- Avoid eyes and broken skin unless specifically instructed.
