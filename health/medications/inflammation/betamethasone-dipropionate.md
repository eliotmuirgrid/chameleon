# Betamethasone Dipropionate

## What it is

Betamethasone dipropionate is a topical corticosteroid used to reduce skin inflammation, redness, and itch.

## How we think it might work

It suppresses local inflammatory and immune signaling in skin, which lowers swelling, redness, and itch.

## Typical dosage ranges (general)

Topical steroid dosing is usually based on amount and frequency, not oral milligrams.

Common use pattern in practice:

- apply a thin layer once or twice daily,
- use the lowest amount needed,
- use short courses when possible, then reassess.

## Known negative side effects

Common local side effects:

- burning, stinging, dryness, or irritation,
- acne-like rash or folliculitis.

Risk with prolonged/repeated use:

- skin thinning (`atrophy`),
- stretch marks (`striae`),
- visible small blood vessels (`telangiectasia`),
- skin color change.

Sexual side effects:

- Sexual side effects are not a common primary effect of topical betamethasone dipropionate.

## Important safety notes

- This is general information, not a personal treatment plan.
- Use only as directed by a licensed clinician.
- Avoid long-term continuous use unless specifically supervised.
- Avoid eyes, face, groin, and skin folds unless specifically instructed.
