# Diclofenac Topical Patch/Plaster

## What it is

Diclofenac is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID).

Topical diclofenac patch/plaster products are used for local pain and inflammation (for example muscle strain, sprain, or localized soft-tissue pain).

## How we think it might work

Diclofenac inhibits cyclooxygenase pathways (`COX`), reducing prostaglandin production in local tissue.

That can lower pain, swelling, and inflammatory signaling at the application site.

## Typical dosage ranges (general)

Dosing depends on the specific product formulation and local label instructions.

Common topical patch/plaster patterns in practice:

- Apply to intact skin over the painful area.
- Usually changed every `12-24` hours depending on product type.
- Use the shortest duration needed for symptom control.

Always follow the exact product label for:

- number of patches/plasters per day,
- maximum daily amount,
- and maximum treatment duration.

## Known negative side effects

Common side effects:

- local skin irritation, redness, itching, or rash,
- mild burning sensation at application site.

Less common but important:

- systemic NSAID side effects can still occur (especially with high exposure or prolonged use), including stomach irritation, kidney effects, or blood pressure effects,
- allergic reactions (including NSAID hypersensitivity),
- photosensitivity in some topical formulations.

Sexual side effects:

- Sexual side effects are not a common primary effect of topical diclofenac products.

## Important safety notes

- This is general information, not a personal treatment plan.
- Confirm the exact active ingredient, salt form, and strength on your local package.
- Do not apply to broken skin, eyes, or mucosa.
- Avoid combining with other NSAIDs unless advised by a clinician.
