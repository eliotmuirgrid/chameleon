# Calorie deficit

To lose weight, **calories in** must be less than **calories out**.

**Homo sapiens** emerged in **Africa**, in landscapes where food could disappear for stretches at a time. Across that history, bodies that could **stretch a meal** and **live off reserves** were more likely to survive. You still carry that inheritance: when intake falls below what you burn, the system adapts.

In a **calorie deficit**, the body saves energy where it can. It releases **fat** (stored fuel). It can also break down **muscle**—active tissue that costs calories to keep—when the gap is large or prolonged. So weight loss is not “fat only”; it is the whole body responding to scarcity—the same broad pattern that helped ancestors endure when food was hard to find in Africa and beyond.
