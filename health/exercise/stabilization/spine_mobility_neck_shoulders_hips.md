# Spine mobility - neck, shoulders, hips

**Category:** Stabilization

**Cues / notes:** Chin in - imagine holding tennis ball for the neck exercise.

Straighter arms while moving only shoulder blades.

Move hips only - slower movement.

---

To improve control of your spine, shoulders, and hips by teaching each joint to move independently. This builds stability, reduces compensation (cheating through other areas), lowers injury risk, and makes your strength and exercise training more effective.

Spine & Neck: Start on hands and knees. Inhale through nose. Exhale slowly while tucking hips and rounding spine one segment at a time, finishing with chin tucked. Inhale and return slowly to neutral. Move only as fast as your breath. ERICA NOT SURE ABOUT THIS ONE!

Shoulder Blades: Arms stay straight. Inhale and let chest drop slightly (shoulder blades together). Exhale and push floor away (shoulder blades apart). Keep neck and lower back still.

Hips: Back flat, body steady. Inhale and brace core lightly. Exhale and slowly move one leg in a circle. Keep hips and spine completely still. Breathe steadily.

Rules: Exhale = move, inhale = reset. Keep movements slow, small, and controlled. If you lose control, slow down.
