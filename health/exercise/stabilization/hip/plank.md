# Hip Flexor Lunge Mobility – Kneeling & Plank

**Category:** Stabilization

**Cues / notes:** 2 second pause with foot forward.

---

Hip Flexor Mobility (Kneeling → Plank): Start by bringing one foot forward so it is close to your hand, with your back knee on the floor. Keep your chest tall and gently push your hips forward, feeling a stretch in the front of the back hip. Do this slowly 3 times, using your breath—inhale to prepare, exhale as you move forward. Then lift your back knee off the floor into a plank position and repeat the same forward hip drive 3 times, keeping your body stable.

Focus on keeping your front foot flat, your knee tracking over your toes, and your torso upright. Do not arch your lower back—keep a slight pelvic tilt so the stretch stays in the hip flexor rather than the spine. Move in a controlled way and use your breath to guide the motion.

Purpose: This exercise improves hip mobility while teaching your body to stay stable through the core. The kneeling version lets you feel and open the hip flexor gently, while the plank version adds strength and control by forcing your core and hips to stabilize under load. This helps reduce tightness from sitting, improves movement in exercises like lunges and running, and prevents your lower back from compensating for limited hip mobility.
