# Breath exercise

**Category:** Stabilization

---

This exercise is a breathing and control drill designed to retrain how your diaphragm, rib cage, and core work together. You start by inhaling into your belly (diaphragm down), then fully exhale to bring your ribs down into a neutral position. From there, you expand your rib cage without taking in more air, which forces you to control the structure of your torso rather than just relying on airflow.

The key idea is separating breathing from movement. Most people can only expand their ribs by inhaling, but this drill teaches you to move and stabilize your rib cage independently. That improves posture, helps prevent rib flare and lower back overextension, and builds better coordination between your diaphragm and core muscles.

It also develops what's called 360° pressure—even expansion of the torso in all directions (front, sides, and back). This creates a stable "cylinder" around your spine, which improves strength, protects your back, and makes movements like lifting and exercise more efficient.
