# Triceps with Elastic

**Category:** Strength

---

Stand tall holding an elastic band, either anchored overhead or held in both hands (one hand stabilizing, the other working). Keep your posture upright with your core lightly braced and ribs down. Bring your elbows close to your head or sides depending on the variation, and keep them still throughout the movement.

As you breathe out, extend your arms by straightening your elbows, pushing the band away until your arms are fully extended. Focus on squeezing your triceps at the end of the movement. Avoid letting your elbows flare out or your shoulders shrug—only your forearms should move.

Slowly return to the starting position while breathing in, keeping control and tension in the band. Don't let it snap back. Move smoothly and avoid using momentum or leaning your body to help the movement.

Purpose: This exercise strengthens the triceps and improves arm control. The elastic band provides steady resistance through the full range, helping build strength without stressing the joints. It also reinforces good posture and teaches you to isolate the triceps without compensating through the shoulders or back.
