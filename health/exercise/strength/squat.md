# Squat

**Category:** Strength

---

Squat: Stand with your feet about shoulder-width apart and your toes slightly turned out. Before you move, brace your core lightly and keep your chest tall. Start the movement by pushing your hips back slightly, then bend your knees and lower yourself down as if you are sitting into a chair. Keep your weight balanced over your mid-foot, not just your toes.

As you go down, keep your knees tracking in line with your toes and avoid letting them collapse inward. Your back should stay neutral—not overly arched or rounded. Go as low as you can while maintaining control and good form. Breathe in as you lower yourself.

To come back up, press through your feet and straighten your legs, squeezing your glutes at the top. Breathe out as you stand. The goal of the squat is to build strength and control in your hips and legs while teaching your body to move efficiently and safely through a very common movement pattern.
