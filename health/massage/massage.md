# Massage

## Concept

**Massage** is **skilled touch** (hands, forearms, or simple tools) applied to **soft tissue**—mostly **muscles and the tissue around them**—to **change how the body feels and moves**: less tension, **easier range of motion**, or **calmer nerves**. The same idea can be **light or firm**, **short or long**, and it can be **therapeutic, athletic, or simply restorative**; what stays constant is using **contact and pressure** on purpose, not by accident.

## Why it belongs with health

It is a **practical** way to support **comfort**, **recovery after effort**, and **body awareness**—separate from, but **compatible with**, exercise and other care. It does **not** replace medical diagnosis or treatment when something is **injured, infected, or newly severe**; in those cases the right first step is a **clinician**, not a session style.

## Transfer test

If you take “**controlled pressure to change how tissue behaves**” out of a spa, it still makes sense: **foam rolling**, **sports work** before or after hard training, **gentle** hand routines for **stiffness** in daily life, or **rehab** settings where touch is one part of a plan.
