# Bottleneck

**Summary:** A **bottleneck** is the part of a system that **limits how much can flow through**. Work piles up behind it; the rest of the system cannot run faster than that point until something changes.

---

The image is literal: the **narrow neck of a bottle** limits how fast liquid pours out. In **work**, **logistics**, or **daily life**, the bottleneck is whatever step or resource **sets the maximum pace** for the whole chain.

That bottleneck might be a **machine**, a **person**, a **rule**, a **missing piece of information**, or a **slow handoff** between people. Until you **see** it and **address** it, extra effort elsewhere often **does not increase useful output**—it only creates **queues**, **stress**, or **waste**.

---

## Bottleneck and “constraint”

In [theory of constraints](theory_of_constraints.md), the same idea is usually called a **constraint**. **Bottleneck** and **constraint** are **the same role** in the model: the **limiting factor** you want to find first so the **whole system** can improve.
