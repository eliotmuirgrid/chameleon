# Screenshot part of the screen (macOS)

**Goal:** capture **only a rectangle you choose**—not the whole display—using a built-in shortcut. No extra app required.

These steps work on current **macOS** (the name Apple uses today for Mac software; older books said “Mac OS X,” but the idea is the same).

---

## Start a region capture

1. Press and hold **Shift**, **Command (⌘)**, and **4** at the same time.  
2. Release the keys. The pointer turns into a **crosshair** (a small plus sign).

---

## Draw the rectangle

1. Click and hold the **trackpad** or **mouse button** where one corner of your shot should be.  
2. Drag diagonally to the opposite corner so the area you want is inside the box.  
3. Let go. You hear a soft camera shutter sound if sound is on.

To **cancel** without saving anything, press **Escape** before you finish, or press **Escape** after the crosshair appears if you change your mind before dragging.

---

## Where the picture goes

macOS usually saves the image as a **PNG** file on your **Desktop**, with a name like “Screen Shot …”. On some versions a **small preview** appears in a corner first; you can click it to crop or mark up before it is saved, or ignore it and use the file on the Desktop.

---

## If you need more options later

**Shift + Command + 5** opens a **Screenshot** toolbar at the bottom of the screen with on-screen buttons (whole screen, selected window, selected portion, timer, and so on). For a simple region grab, **Shift + Command + 4** is usually enough.
