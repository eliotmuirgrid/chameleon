# Breathing-driven glute bridge with spinal control.

**Category:** Stabilization

**Cues / notes:** Keep hip tilt with raising hip

---

That exercise is essentially a breathing-driven glute bridge with spinal control.

You start by breathing into your rib cage to expand it, then as you exhale you tilt your pelvis (flattening your lower back) and lift your hips. The exhale helps engage your deep core and glutes, while the pelvic tilt ensures you're not overusing your lower back. Then, as you come down slowly vertebrae by vertebrae, you're training controlled spinal movement instead of just dropping.

The purpose is to coordinate breathing, core activation, and movement. It teaches you to use your glutes and core together, improves spinal awareness, and reinforces proper positioning (ribs down, pelvis neutral). Over time, this helps reduce lower back strain and builds a more stable, controlled foundation for lifting and everyday movement.
