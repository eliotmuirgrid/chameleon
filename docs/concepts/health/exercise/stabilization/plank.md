# Plank

**Category:** Stabilization

---

Start by setting your body in a straight line from head to heels. Place your hands under your shoulders for the high plank, or your elbows under your shoulders for the forearm plank. Before you hold the position, do a slight pelvic tilt by tucking your hips under a little. Think about pulling your pubic bone slightly toward your ribs. This helps flatten your lower back and stops it from sagging.

Once you are in position, brace your abs as if you are preparing to be lightly punched in the stomach. Squeeze your glutes, keep your ribs down, and push the floor away so your shoulders stay strong and stable. Your neck should stay long and neutral, with your head in line with your spine rather than hanging down or lifting up. Keep breathing steadily and do not hold your breath.

The purpose of the plank is not just to "hold still," but to teach your body how to create full-body tension and stability. The pelvic tilt is important because it helps put your spine in a safer, stronger position and makes your abs do the work instead of dumping the load into your lower back. When done properly, the plank trains your core to support movement, protects your back, and improves control for many other exercises.
