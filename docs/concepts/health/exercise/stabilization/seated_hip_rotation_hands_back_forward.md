# Seated Hip Rotation – Hands Back & Forward

**Category:** Stabilization

---

Seated Hip Rotation – Hands Back & Forward: Sit on the floor with your knees bent and feet planted, slightly wider than hip width. Start with your hands placed behind you for support. Keeping your feet in contact with the ground, slowly drop both knees to one side, then bring them back up and over to the other side. Move in a controlled, side-to-side motion, aiming to get your knees closer to the floor each time without forcing it. Breathe steadily—inhale as you come to center, exhale as your knees move to each side.

After a few reps, bring your arms forward in front of you instead of behind. This removes some support and makes the movement more challenging. You'll need to use your core and hips more to control the motion and stay upright. Keep your chest tall and avoid collapsing or twisting excessively through your spine—the movement should come mainly from your hips.

Purpose: This exercise improves hip rotation, especially internal rotation, which is often limited from sitting and daily habits. The hands-back version helps you explore the movement with support, while the arms-forward version builds strength and control in that range. Better hip rotation reduces strain on the knees and lower back, improves movement in exercises like squats and lunges, and helps your body move more efficiently without compensating.
