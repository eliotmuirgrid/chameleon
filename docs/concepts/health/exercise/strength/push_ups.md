# Push ups

**Category:** Strength

---

Push-Ups (Standard, Knees, Single-Leg): Start with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width and your body in a straight line from head to heels (or from head to knees if doing them on all fours). Before you begin, lightly brace your core and keep a small pelvic tilt so your lower back doesn't sag. Your neck stays neutral—look slightly ahead, not straight down.

As you lower yourself, bend your elbows and keep them at about a 30–45 degree angle from your body (not flaring out wide). Lower in a controlled way until your chest gets close to the floor. Breathe in as you go down. Keep your body moving as one unit—avoid letting your hips drop or your shoulders shrug up.

Push through your hands to return to the top, straightening your arms while maintaining that straight body line. Breathe out as you push up. If doing the easier version on your knees, focus on the same alignment and control. If doing the harder version with one leg raised, keep your hips level and avoid twisting—this adds a stability challenge for your core.

Purpose: Push-ups build upper body strength in the chest, shoulders, and arms while also training your core to stabilize your body. The knee version helps you learn proper form with less load, while the single-leg version increases difficulty by adding balance and control demands. Done carefully, push-ups improve strength, posture, and coordination across your whole body.
