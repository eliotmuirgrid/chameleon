# Lunges

**Category:** Strength

**Cues / notes:** Breath out as you go down and up because both require exertion

---

Start standing tall with your feet hip-width apart. Step one foot forward into a split stance. Before lowering, lightly brace your core and keep your chest upright. As you bend both knees, lower your body straight down—your front knee should track over your toes, and your back knee moves toward the floor. Breathe in as you go down, keeping your balance steady and your weight centered.

At the bottom, both legs should be working—front leg supporting most of the load, back leg helping with balance. Keep your torso tall and avoid leaning forward or arching your lower back. Your front heel stays grounded. To come back up, press through your front foot and return to standing. Breathe out as you rise.

If you need an easier version, you can do supported lunges by holding onto something for balance or doing smaller steps. For a more advanced version, you can slow the movement down, add a pause at the bottom, or keep one leg elevated or moving continuously. The goal is always control, not speed.

Purpose: Lunges build strength and stability in your legs and hips while also challenging balance and coordination. They train each leg independently, helping correct imbalances and improving movement for walking, running, and other daily activities. Done properly, they also teach your body to control alignment and reduce strain on your knees and lower back.
