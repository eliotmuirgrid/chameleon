# Side plank - left and right

**Category:** Strength

---

Start by lying on your side with your elbow directly under your shoulder and your legs stacked. Before lifting, set your body by bracing your core and lightly stacking your ribs over your hips. Press your forearm into the floor and lift your hips so your body forms a straight line from head to feet. Keep your neck neutral and your shoulders stable—not collapsing into the joint.

Hold the position while breathing steadily. Think about lifting your bottom hip up and slightly forward, and keep your top hip stacked directly above the bottom one. Avoid letting your body rotate forward or backward. Your core and glutes should stay engaged the whole time. If needed, you can bend your bottom knee for an easier version while keeping the same alignment.

To come down, lower your hips slowly with control. Repeat on both sides. If you want to make it harder, you can straighten both legs fully, raise the top leg slightly, or extend your top arm upward while maintaining balance.

Purpose: Side planks build strength in the side core (obliques) and improve lateral stability. They help your body resist rotation and keep your spine aligned during movement. This reduces strain on the lower back and improves control for exercises like running, lifting, and everyday movements where balance and stability matter.
