# Shoulder Blades with Elastic

**Category:** Strength

---

Stand tall holding an elastic band with both hands at about shoulder height. Keep your arms mostly straight with a slight bend in the elbows. Before you start, brace your core lightly and keep your ribs down so your lower back doesn't arch. Your shoulders should stay relaxed—not shrugged up.

Pull the band apart by moving your shoulder blades, not just your arms. Think about drawing your shoulder blades back and slightly down as you open your arms. Breathe out as you pull. Keep your chest tall and avoid flaring your ribs or leaning back. The movement should be controlled and come from your upper back, not your neck.

Slowly return to the starting position while breathing in, keeping tension in the band the whole time. Don't let it snap back. Move with control and focus on smooth, steady motion. You can vary the height (chest level, overhead, or lower) to target slightly different areas.

Purpose: This exercise strengthens the muscles around your shoulder blades and improves posture. It teaches your shoulders to move properly without relying on your neck or lower back. This helps reduce shoulder pain, improves stability for pressing and pulling exercises, and supports better alignment in everyday movements.
