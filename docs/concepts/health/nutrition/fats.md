**Fats: Structural, Hormonal, and Regulatory Fuel**

Fats are not optional—they are foundational to how the body is built and regulated.

At the most basic level, fats are **structural**. Every cell membrane is made from fat, and the brain relies heavily on it for signaling. Without adequate fat, cellular integrity and communication begin to degrade.

Fats are also **hormonal inputs**. Many key hormones are synthesized from cholesterol and fatty acids. Low fat intake can disrupt hormone production, affecting energy, mood, and recovery.

They enable **nutrient access**. Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) require dietary fat to be absorbed. Without it, even a well-designed diet becomes inefficient.

Functionally, fats provide **stable energy** and **satiety**. They burn slowly, helping regulate hunger and smooth out energy levels over time.

The principle is simple: fats are not just fuel—they are part of the system itself. The goal is not elimination, but balance and quality.
