# Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the body’s most efficient fuel for effort: they deliver quick energy to the brain and muscles when you need to think and move hard.

In a [calorie deficit](calorie_deficit.md), keeping enough carbohydrate helps preserve muscle. When carb intake is too low, the body scrambles for glucose and can lean on protein to make it—working against muscle you might want to keep. Adequate carbs reduce that pressure.

They also refill glycogen after demanding work, support recovery, and sit alongside sleep, mood, and stress physiology—systems that suffer when fuel is chronically thin.

The aim is not the highest possible carbs, but sufficient carbs. Cutting them out entirely usually hurts how you perform and recover. Used in the right amount, carbohydrates are a stabilizing input: they support output, recovery, and consistency day to day.
