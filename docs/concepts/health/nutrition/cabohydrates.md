Carbohydrates as a System Input

Carbohydrates are the body’s most efficient fuel for effort, supplying quick energy to both the brain and muscles. In a calorie deficit, maintaining adequate carbohydrate intake helps preserve muscle by reducing the need to convert protein into glucose.

Carbs also support key system functions: they replenish glycogen for recovery, help regulate stress hormones like cortisol, and contribute to better sleep and emotional stability.

The goal is not high carbs, but sufficient carbs. Removing them entirely degrades system performance. Used properly, carbohydrates act as a stabilizing energy input that improves output, recovery, and consistency.
