# Pipeline

**What it is:** Things happen **one after another**. Step two uses what step one produced. Step three uses what step two produced. There is a clear **first** step and a clear **last** step.

**When something goes wrong:** Ask: **where did it first go wrong?** Fix that spot first. You do not have to fix the whole chain at once.

**Picture:** Water in a hose. If nothing comes out the end, trace the path. Find the **first** place where flow stops—a kink, a closed valve, a leak. Fix that. Often the rest starts working again.

**Why this helps:** You get a **single place to look** instead of guessing everywhere at once.

**Reality check:** Sometimes two things happen at the same time. Sometimes work goes **back** to an earlier step. A straight line is still a good **first** map; you can add detail later.
