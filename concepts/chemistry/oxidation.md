# Oxidation

**Oxidation** is a chemical process in which a substance **loses electrons** (often, but not always, by **reacting with oxygen**). The partner process is **reduction** (gain of electrons); together they are **redox** reactions.

In everyday language, “oxidation” often means **corrosion or tarnish**: iron **rusts**, copper **greens**, aluminum forms a **dull oxide skin**, and even **stainless steel** can show **surface staining or rough oxide** where the passive layer is disturbed or contaminants sit on the metal.

That matters for **household metal** because oxidation usually starts at the **surface**. It can be **mostly cosmetic** (a film you can clean or cover) or **structural** (deep rust eating the metal). The same word connects **chemistry** to **cleaning and maintenance**.
