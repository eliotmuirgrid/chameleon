# CO2 Solubility and Temperature

**Carbon dioxide (CO2) dissolves better in cold water than in warm water.** As temperature rises, water molecules move faster and interact less effectively with dissolved gas, so CO2 escapes more easily into the air.

That is why **warm water loses CO2 faster**: it cannot hold as much dissolved gas as cold water can.

Simple way to think about it:

- **Cold water holds more CO2** -> stays fizzy
- **Warm water holds less CO2** -> goes flat faster

So if you want to keep bubbles, keep it cold. If you want them gone, warm it up.
