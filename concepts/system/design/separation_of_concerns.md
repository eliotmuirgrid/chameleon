# Separation of concerns

Systems that **separate** concerns **cleanly**—in a **logical**, **simple** way—are **easier to understand**, **simpler to maintain**, and less likely to **slide into disorder** over time—related to [**entropy**](../../pareto/general/decluttering_and_retrieval.md#entropy-and-search-space) (things drifting toward mess unless you push back).

Think of it as giving everything its **own home**, so you can reach for **one** thing without making a mess of the rest.

**Kitchen:** forks in a drawer, milk in the fridge, plates in a cupboard. Because each kind of thing has a place:

- **Safer** — A spill stays **local**: milk in the fridge does not soak the fork drawer—or the mail on the counter.
- **Easier to fix** — You can replace the spoons without accidentally breaking a glass.
- **Easier to learn** — You can tell a guest “the glasses are here” without narrating where every snack and bill lives too.

When **different needs** live in **different spots**, you can **change one corner** of the room without **ruining the rest**. The same **one home for one job** idea shows up in **software** and in **shared plans**—the pages below spell it out in the abstract, then in **code** and **diet reports**.
