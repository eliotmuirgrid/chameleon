# Simple English (80/20 language rule)

Use the smallest set of words that most people understand.

Rule:

- Use the **20% of language** that **80% of people** understand.
- Prefer **high-school English**.
- Avoid specialist words unless you must use them.
- If you must use one, define it in one short line.

## Why this reduces cognitive load

- Fewer unknown words means less mental effort.
- The reader spends energy on the idea, not decoding terms.
- More people can follow the same page with less friction.
- Recursive simplification helps until meaning starts to drop; that is an [entropy](../entropy.md) warning point.

## Quick writing test

Before you keep a sentence, ask:

1. Can a bright high-school student understand it?
2. Can I replace a complex word with a common word?
3. Can I make the sentence shorter without losing meaning?

If yes, simplify it.

## Example

- Hard: "Baseline normalization minimizes extraneous variance in observed measurements."
- Simple: "Set a clear zero point so readings show what changed, not background noise."
