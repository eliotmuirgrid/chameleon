# Equilibrium example - Eliot assimilating new concepts

When Eliot takes in many new ideas, the cognitive inflow can temporarily exceed his processing and integration speed.

If inflow stays too high for too long, internal queues grow, and that can feel like confusion, fatigue, and reduced clarity.

For stability, Eliot needs enough space (time, fewer interruptions, and smaller packets) so understanding can catch up gently.

That creates a personal [equilibrium](../equilibrium.md): concept intake and concept integration stay in a workable balance.

Eliot can signal this kindly by wearing his AirPods when he is at his computer.

Without this space, Eliot as a system can become less stable.
