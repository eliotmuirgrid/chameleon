# Symmetry (system design)

**Symmetry** here means: **the two sides match.** Same kind of pieces, same kind of rules, so once you learn one side you mostly know the other.

**Why bother:** Less to remember. Fewer mistakes. Fixes and habits transfer.

**When the sides should not match:** When the job really is different (speed vs safety, old system vs new, different laws or users). Then make the difference **obvious on purpose**, not hidden.

**Quick test:** Could you explain the second half in one sentence as “like the first half, except …”? If you need a whole new story, symmetry is weak.

## Examples

- Two people each keep their own food log, but the file names and columns line up—`targets_eliot.tsv` and `targets_subrina.tsv`—so you are not learning two different systems.
