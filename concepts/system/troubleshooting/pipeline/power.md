# Power Pipeline (example: computer won’t turn on)

This page is one **example** of [thinking in a pipeline](../pipeline.md): **electricity has to reach the computer** before anything else can work. If there is **no power**, the rest of the system does not matter yet.

## First question: does it have power?

**Symptom:** The computer does **nothing** when you press the power button—no lights, no fans, no sounds.

**Start at the beginning of the chain** (power coming **in** from the wall):

1. **Is the wall outlet live?** Plug in a lamp or phone charger you **know** works. If that device does not turn on either, the problem is **not** inside the computer—it is the **outlet**, the **breaker**, or a **house** issue.  Some power outlets have light indicators showing power.
2. **Is the power strip or surge protector on?** Some have a **switch**. Some have a **breaker button** that pops out after a surge—press it to reset.
3. **Is the power cable firmly seated** at **both** ends (wall or strip **and** the back of the computer or power brick)?
4. **If there is a separate power brick** (many laptops): is the **small** plug into the laptop **fully** in? Is the **LED** on the brick lit (if it has one)?
5. Are all the connections secure?

Only after **power reaches the machine** does it make sense to worry about the **power button**, the **supply inside the box**, or the **motherboard**. Those are **later** steps in the chain.

## Why this order matters

A pipeline fails at the **first** broken step. If no electricity enters, you can replace parts inside the case and still see nothing. Checking **power first** saves time and money.
