# Slatboard (open home system)

A slatboard is a physical example of an open system.

Using a slatboard in a walk-in closet with Subrina shows a bigger pattern: when you adopt open systems at home, rooms become easier to adapt over time.

Instead of treating a room as a fixed final product, you treat it as a modular system.

The slatboard is a stable mounting layer (an interface) where mirrors, lighting, power cables, shelves, and accessories can be added, moved, and replaced as needs change.

This removes pressure to get everything perfect up front, because the system is designed to evolve.

A useful side effect: components can deliver value before a "final install." A mirror mounted on the board is already usable, testable, and movable from day one.

In system terms, this is open because parts are interchangeable and the arrangement can evolve through [iteration](../system/algorithms/iteration.md), not one irreversible build.

This shifts the mindset from "finish a room" to "evolve a system."
