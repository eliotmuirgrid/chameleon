# Mountain Climbers to Chest

**Category:** Strength

---

Mountain Climbers to Chest (Alternating): Start in a strong plank position with your hands under your shoulders and your body in a straight line from head to heels. Set a slight pelvic tilt and brace your core so your lower back doesn't sag. Keep your shoulders stable by pushing the floor away and your neck neutral.

From this position, bring one knee forward toward your chest while breathing out, keeping your hips level and your body steady. Then switch legs, alternating in a controlled rhythm. Move one leg at a time rather than bouncing—focus on clean, deliberate steps. Your upper body should stay as still as possible.

Continue alternating knees while breathing steadily. If you go faster, maintain control—don't let your hips bounce or your lower back arch. If needed, slow it down to keep the movement clean and stable.

Purpose: This exercise builds core strength, hip mobility, and coordination while also adding a cardio element. It trains your body to stabilize the spine while the legs move, which is important for running, athletic movement, and overall control. Done properly, it improves endurance and reinforces strong plank positioning under movement.
