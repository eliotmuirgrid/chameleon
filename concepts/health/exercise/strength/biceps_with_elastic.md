# Biceps with elastic

**Category:** Strength

---

Stand tall with the middle of the elastic band under your feet and one end in each hand. Keep your posture upright with your chest tall, ribs down, and core lightly braced. Let your arms hang by your sides with your palms facing forward. Keep your elbows close to your body and still—this is important.

As you breathe out, curl your hands up toward your shoulders by bending your elbows. Focus on squeezing your biceps at the top without letting your elbows drift forward or your shoulders lift. The movement should be controlled and isolated to the arms. Avoid leaning back or using momentum to help the lift.

Slowly lower your hands back down while breathing in, keeping tension in the band the whole time. Don't let it snap back. Move smoothly and stay in control throughout the full range. If needed, adjust the band tension or your stance to keep the movement clean.

Purpose: This exercise strengthens the biceps while teaching controlled, isolated arm movement. Using an elastic band provides constant tension through the whole motion, which helps build strength and control without stressing the joints. It also reinforces good posture and prevents compensations like swinging or using the shoulders.
