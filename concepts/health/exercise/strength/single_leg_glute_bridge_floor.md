# Single-Leg Glute Bridge (Floor)

**Category:** Strength

---

Start lying on your back with one foot planted on the floor and the other leg lifted. Your arms can rest by your sides for support. Before you move, gently tuck your hips (pelvic tilt) so your lower back presses lightly into the floor. Brace your core and keep your ribs down.

Press through your planted foot and lift your hips up, squeezing your glute as you rise. Breathe out as you lift. At the top, your body should form a straight line from shoulders to knee on the working leg. Keep your hips level—don't let one side drop or twist. Avoid pushing through your lower back; the movement should come from your hip.

Lower your hips back down slowly while breathing in, maintaining control the whole way. Move steadily and focus on feeling the glute doing the work. If it feels unstable, you can start with both feet on the ground and progress to single-leg as your control improves.

Purpose: This exercise strengthens the glutes and improves single-leg stability. It helps correct left-right imbalances and teaches proper hip extension without relying on the lower back. This carries over to movements like walking, running, lunges, and overall posture.
