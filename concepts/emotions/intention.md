# Intention

**Intention** is what you **mean to do** before or while you act—not every habit on autopilot, and not only what you *wish* would happen, but the **aim** you hold in mind when you choose.

**Why it matters:** Actions line up better with values when there is a **clear aim**. Without intention, behavior drifts: you react, copy, or repeat what felt easiest last time. With intention, you can **notice** that gap between aim and outcome and **adjust**.

**Intention vs outcome:** You control **effort and honesty of aim** more than **results**. Weather, other people, and luck still matter. Intention is not a promise that the world will comply—it is **steering**, not a guarantee.

**Practice:** Name the aim in **simple words** (“I mean to listen,” “I mean to finish this draft”). Small, spoken or written intentions **stick** better than vague moods. They pair well with [attention](how_consciousness_can_be_raised.md) and with checking whether what you did **matched** what you meant.
