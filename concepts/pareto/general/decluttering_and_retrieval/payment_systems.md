# Decluttering and retrieval — payment systems example

## Concept

Decluttering in payment systems means removing methods that no longer work and clearly labeling the methods that remain.

## Example

A payment flow may accumulate stale options over time (expired cards, disabled processors, legacy methods).

Apply decluttering by:

1. Removing expired or invalid payment techniques.
2. Keeping a small active set of valid methods.
3. Labeling each method clearly so people can pick quickly.

## Outcome

- Faster checkout decisions.
- Fewer payment errors.
- Better retrieval: users can find a valid method without scanning noise.
