# Decluttering and retrieval

**Summary:** Decluttering is not mainly about owning less. It is about finding what you need, when you need it.

---

## A hard rule

If you cannot find something when you need it, remove it.

If you cannot retrieve it, it has no practical value.

Here, *index* means a reliable way back to it:

- a fixed place,
- a clear label,
- a list,
- or a habit of returning it to the same spot.

---

## What “keeping it just in case” assumes

When we keep things "just in case," we assume we will find them later.

That often fails.

Without a reliable way to retrieve an item, it is effectively lost, even if it is still nearby.

---

## Entropy and search space

The deeper issue is [entropy](../../system/design/entropy.md): things drift toward disorder over time.

Each extra item increases the number of places something could be.

Search becomes slower and less certain.

Small "just in case" choices add up, and useful things get buried in noise.

Decluttering pushes back on that drift.

Fewer items means:

- fewer places to search,
- a clearer space,
- and retrieval you can trust.

---

## Having vs storing

If you cannot find something quickly and reliably, you do not really have it.

You are only storing it.
