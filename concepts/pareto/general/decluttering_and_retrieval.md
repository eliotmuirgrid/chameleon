# Decluttering and retrieval

**Summary:** Decluttering is not mainly about owning **less**. It is about being able to **find what you need** when you need it.

---

## A hard rule

**Eliminate what you cannot index.** If you cannot **retrieve** it when you need it, it has **zero value**—not “low” value: **none**. *Index* means any **reliable** way back to the thing: a place, a label, a list, a habit of return. Without that, ownership is only **cost** and **clutter**.

---

## What “keeping it just in case” assumes

When we keep things because we **might** need them, we assume our **future selves** will **locate** them. That assumption often fails. Without a **reliable way to retrieve** an item, it is **effectively lost**, even if it is still in the building.

---

## Entropy and search space

The deeper issue is **entropy**: things drift toward **disorder** on their own. Every extra item increases the number of **places** something could be, so search gets **slower** and **less certain**. Small “just in case” choices **compound**; useful things end up **buried in noise**.

**Decluttering** pushes back on that entropy. Fewer items means a **smaller search space**, **clearer** surroundings, and **retrieval** you can trust again.

---

## Having vs storing

If you cannot **quickly and consistently find** something, you do not really **have** it—you are only **storing** it.
